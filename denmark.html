DENMARK


5.9 Million inhabitants Copenhagen 2:nd place in WHR 2022

World Happiness Report 2022


Denmark ranks second in the world in terms of happiness, with a score of 75.9 out of 100, according to the World Happiness Report 2022. Denmark's high happiness rating is due to several factors, including a strong social support network, high levels of personal freedom and civil liberties, and a high GDP per capita. Denmark also has a low level of corruption, likely due to its strong rule of law and transparent government institutions. This fosters trust in the government, which may help citizens feel more secure and content in their daily lives. Denmark's high happiness score reflects the country's social and political stability and commitment to ensuring its citizens' well-being.

Denmark: Consistent High Happiness Scores


Denmark has maintained an impressive level of happiness over the last decade, ranging from 75.1 in 2014 and 2015 to 77 in 2021. Denmark's happiness score, on the other hand, fell slightly in 2020. This decrease can be attributed to the COVID-19 pandemic, which had a significant impact on the world's happiness and well-being. To combat the spread of the virus, Denmark, like other countries, implemented measures such as social distancing, lockdowns, and increased hygiene protocols. While necessary for public health, these measures had a negative impact on happiness and well-being. People had to adjust to new ways of working, learning, and socializing, which increased anxiety and stress.
