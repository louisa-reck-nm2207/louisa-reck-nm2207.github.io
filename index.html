What is the World Happiness Report?


The World Happiness Report is an annual survey conducted by the United Nations that ranks countries based on several factors that contribute to well-being and happiness. The report provides valuable insights into the quality of life in different countries and can be useful for policymakers and researchers to identify the factors that contribute to happiness and well-being.


the countries consistently ranking highly in the report are Sweden, Norway, Iceland, Denmark, and Finland. These countries are interesting to compare and examine in more detail due to their high happiness scores and shared characteristics, such as strong social welfare systems, gender equality, high levels of trust in public institutions, and a focus on environmental sustainability. By studying these countries, we can learn about the policies and practices that contribute to their success in promoting happiness and well-being, and apply these insights to other countries to help improve quality of life around the world.
