The World Happiness Report


The World Happiness Report is an annual survey conducted by the United Nations that ranks countries based on a variety of factors that influence happiness and well-being. These factors are GDP per capita, social support, helathy life expectancy, freedom to make life choices, generosity and freedom from corruption.The report offers useful insights into the quality of life in various countries and can help policymakers and researchers identify the factors that contribute to happiness.


Some countries that consistently rank high in the report are the northern European countries Sweden, Norway, Denmark, Finland and Iceland. Because of their high happiness scores and shared characteristics, such as strong social welfare systems, gender equality, high levels of trust in public institutions, and a focus on environmental sustainability, these countries are interesting to compare and examine in greater depth. By studying these countries, we can learn about the policies and practices contributing to happiness. Furthermore, we can apply these insights to other countries to help improve the global quality of life.
