World Happiness Report 2022


According to the World Happiness Report 2022, Iceland ranked as the 3:rd happiest country in the world, with a score of 75.3 out of 100. The country's high happiness score can be attributed to various factors such as a strong sense of community, high levels of social support, and a focus on work-life balance. Iceland has even implemented a successful four-day workweek trial that has helped employees reduce stress and achieve better work-life balance. However, the country scored low in the freedom from corruption category of the report. It's important to note that this does not necessarily mean that Iceland is corrupt, as the category measures people's perception of corruption rather than actual corruption levels.

Iceland: A small but happy country


Iceland's happiness index has remained relatively stable over the last decade, rising from 75 in 2013 to 75.3 in 2022*. Even the COVID-19 pandemic had no effect on the country's happiness, as Iceland maintained its high happiness score throughout the pandemic. The country's small population and a strong emphasis on family and social connections contribute to its high score. Iceland also has a high level of gender equality and a focus on work-life balance, with policies like generous parental leave and flexible work hours.
